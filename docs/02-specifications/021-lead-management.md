# PART A — Product Specification

# 1. Purpose

The Lead Management module manages qualified prospective clients after the intake process.

A Lead represents a qualified business opportunity that is actively being pursued by the firm.

Leads are the central business object used before a person becomes a Client.

This module is responsible for:

* Lead lifecycle
* Lead qualification
* Lead assignment
* Lead communication history
* Lead notes
* Lead activities
* Lead conversion

A Lead may eventually become a Client through the Client Management module.

---

# 5. Business Requirements

## LEAD-001

Every lead belongs to exactly one firm.

---

## LEAD-002

Every lead receives a unique Lead Number generated by the system.

The numbering strategy is configurable at the firm level.

---

## LEAD-003

A lead may be created by:

* Converting an Intake
* Manual creation

---

## LEAD-004

Every lead must have an owner.

Ownership may be reassigned.

---

## LEAD-005

Every lead progresses through a defined lifecycle.

Allowed statuses:

* New
* Contacted
* Consultation Scheduled
* Consultation Completed
* Engagement Sent
* Qualified
* Lost
* Converted

---

## LEAD-006

Each intake may create only one lead.

Duplicate lead creation from the same intake is prohibited.

---

## LEAD-007

A lead may be converted into exactly one client.

Once converted:

* Lead becomes read-only.
* Lead cannot be converted again.

---

## LEAD-008

Lost leads remain permanently available for reporting.

Lost leads are never deleted.

---

## LEAD-009

Internal notes and activities may be recorded against a lead.

These records remain internal to the firm.

---

## LEAD-010

Every significant lead event must generate an Audit Log entry.

Examples include:

* Lead Created
* Lead Assigned
* Status Changed
* Consultation Scheduled
* Engagement Sent
* Lead Converted
* Lead Lost

# 12. Security Considerations

This module follows:

* 005 - Security Standards

The following security requirements are specific to the Lead Management module.

---

## Firm Isolation

Every lead belongs to exactly one firm.

Users may only access leads belonging to their own firm.

Cross-firm access is prohibited.

---

## Ownership

Every lead must have exactly one owner.

Only authorized users may reassign ownership.

Ownership changes must be recorded in the Lead Activity history.

---

## Status Transitions

Lead status transitions must follow the approved lifecycle.

Invalid transitions must be rejected.

Once a lead reaches **CONVERTED**, it becomes immutable.

---

## Conversion

A lead may be converted into a Client exactly once.

Successful conversion must:

* Create exactly one Client.
* Link the Lead to the Client.
* Prevent duplicate conversions.
* Preserve all historical lead information.

---

## Attachments

Uploaded files must:

* Validate file type.
* Validate file size.
* Be stored in object storage.

MongoDB stores attachment metadata only.

---

## Internal Information

Lead notes and activities are internal.

They must never be exposed to prospective clients.

---

## Auditability

Every significant lead action must generate an Audit Log entry.

Examples include:

* Lead Created
* Lead Updated
* Lead Assigned
* Consultation Scheduled
* Consultation Completed
* Engagement Sent
* Lead Converted
* Lead Lost
