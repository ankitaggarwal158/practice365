# PART A — Product Specification

---

# 1. Purpose

The Client Management module manages all individuals and organizations that have formally engaged the firm for legal services.

A Client is created only after:

* A Lead has successfully completed the intake process.
* A Conflict Check has been completed and cleared.
* The firm has decided to accept the engagement.

A Client becomes the permanent legal relationship between the firm and the person or organization.

Every Matter within Practice365 belongs to a Client.

# 5. Business Requirements

## CLIENT-001

Every client belongs to exactly one firm.

---

## CLIENT-002

Every client receives a unique Client Number generated by the system.

The numbering strategy is configurable at the firm level.

---

## CLIENT-003

A client may be created only after:

* Lead qualification.
* Successful conflict clearance.

Direct client creation may be permitted only for authorized users.

---

## CLIENT-004

Each client is either:

* Individual
* Organization

The client type cannot be changed after creation.

---

## CLIENT-005

Every client progresses through a lifecycle.

Allowed statuses:

* Active
* Inactive
* Archived

Clients are never permanently deleted.

---

## CLIENT-006

A client may own multiple matters.

Every matter belongs to exactly one client.

---

## CLIENT-007

Multiple contacts may be associated with an organization client.

Support for contact management will be implemented in a future module.

---

## CLIENT-008

Duplicate clients should be avoided.

The system should assist users by displaying potential duplicates during client creation.

---

## CLIENT-009

Clients may be archived but historical legal records must always remain accessible.

---

## CLIENT-010

Every significant client event must generate an Audit Log entry.

Examples include:

* Client Created
* Client Updated
* Client Archived
* Client Reactivated
* Client Merged

---

# 12. Security Considerations

This module follows:

* 005 - Security Standards

The following security requirements are specific to the Client Management module.

---

## Firm Isolation

Every client belongs to exactly one firm.

Users may only access clients belonging to their own firm.

Cross-firm access is strictly prohibited.

---

## Client Creation

A client may be created only if:

* The Lead exists.
* The Lead belongs to the same firm.
* A successful Conflict Check exists.
* The Conflict Check decision is **CLEARED**.

These validations are mandatory.

---

## Immutable Client Type

Once created, the client type cannot be changed.

Examples:

* Individual → Organization ❌
* Organization → Individual ❌

A new client record must be created instead.

---

## Archiving

Clients are never permanently deleted.

Archiving must never affect:

* Matters
* Documents
* Time Entries
* Invoices
* Audit Logs

Historical records must remain accessible.

---

## Client Merge

Client merge is irreversible.

The merge operation must:

* Reassign all dependent records.
* Preserve historical references.
* Record a complete audit trail.

The source client becomes archived after a successful merge.

---

## Duplicate Detection

The system should identify potential duplicates using:

* Email
* Phone Number
* Client Name
* Organization Name

Duplicate detection is advisory only.

The final decision belongs to the user.

---

## Attachments

Uploaded files must:

* Validate file type.
* Validate file size.
* Be stored in object storage.

MongoDB stores metadata only.

---

## Auditability

Every significant client action must generate an Audit Log entry.

Examples include:

* Client Created
* Client Updated
* Client Archived
* Client Reactivated
* Client Merged

---
