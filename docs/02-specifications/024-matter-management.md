# PART A — Product Specification

# 1. Purpose

The Matter Management module is the core business module of Practice365.

A Matter represents a legal engagement between the firm and a client.

Every piece of legal work performed within Practice365 ultimately belongs to a Matter.

All downstream modules including:

* Documents
* Notes
* Tasks
* Calendar
* Time Entries
* Expenses
* Billing
* Trust Accounting
* Emails
* Court Dates
* Reports

reference a Matter.

This module establishes the primary business entity used throughout the remainder of the application.

# 5. Business Requirements

## MATTER-001

Every matter belongs to exactly one firm.

---

## MATTER-002

Every matter belongs to exactly one client (or multiple clients for joint matters, e.g., divorce, co-defendants).

A client may own multiple matters.

---

## MATTER-003

Every matter receives a unique Matter Number generated by the system.

The numbering strategy is configurable at the firm level.

Matter Numbers are immutable.

---

## MATTER-004

Every matter has one Responsible Attorney.

Additional users may participate through the Matter Team.

---

## MATTER-005

Every matter belongs to exactly one Practice Area.

Examples include:

* Civil Litigation
* Criminal Law
* Family Law
* Corporate Law
* Real Estate
* Employment Law
* Immigration
* Estate Planning

Practice Areas are configurable by the firm.

---

## MATTER-006

Every matter progresses through a defined lifecycle.

Allowed statuses:

* Open
* On Hold
* Closed
* Archived

Status transitions are controlled by the system.

---

## MATTER-007

Every matter has a priority.

Allowed priorities:

* Low
* Normal
* High
* Urgent

---

## MATTER-008

A matter may be assigned to multiple firm users.

Assignments define participation but do not change ownership.

---

## MATTER-009

Closed matters remain permanently available.

Historical matters are never deleted.

---

## MATTER-010

Archiving a matter must never delete:

* Documents
* Notes
* Calendar Events
* Time Entries
* Invoices
* Audit Logs

---

## MATTER-011

Every significant matter event must generate an Audit Log entry.

Examples include:

* Matter Created
* Matter Updated
* Matter Assigned
* Attorney Changed
* Status Changed
* Matter Closed
* Matter Archived
* Matter Reopened

# 12. Security Considerations

This module follows:

* 005 - Security Standards

The following security requirements are specific to the Matter Management module.

---

## Firm Isolation

Every matter belongs to exactly one firm.

Users may only access matters belonging to their own firm.

Cross-firm access is strictly prohibited.

---

## Client Ownership

Every matter must belong to exactly one client.

Changing the client associated with an existing matter is prohibited.

If legal representation changes, a new matter must be created.

---

## Responsible Attorney

Every matter must always have exactly one Responsible Attorney.

Changing the Responsible Attorney:

* Does not change matter ownership.
* Must be recorded in the audit history.
* Must preserve historical responsibility records.

---

## Matter Team

Only users belonging to the same firm may be assigned to a matter.

Duplicate team assignments are prohibited.

Removing a user from the Matter Team must never affect historical records created by that user.

---

## Matter Lifecycle

Only valid lifecycle transitions are permitted.

Allowed transitions include:

```text
OPEN
    ↓
ON_HOLD
    ↓
OPEN
    ↓
CLOSED
    ↓
ARCHIVED
```

Archived matters may only be restored by authorized users.

Closed or archived matters remain available for reporting and historical reference.

---

## Immutable Fields

The following fields become immutable after matter creation:

* Matter Number
* Firm
* Client
* Conflict Check Reference
* Original Lead Reference

These values must never be modified.

---

## Archive

Archiving a matter must never remove or modify:

* Documents
* Notes
* Calendar Events
* Tasks
* Time Entries
* Expenses
* Invoices
* Payments
* Audit Logs

The matter becomes read-only unless explicitly reopened.

---

## Attachments

Uploaded files must:

* Validate type.
* Validate size.
* Be stored using the platform document storage service.

MongoDB stores metadata only.

---

## Auditability

Every significant matter action must generate an Audit Log entry.

Examples include:

* Matter Created
* Matter Updated
* Responsible Attorney Changed
* Team Updated
* Matter Closed
* Matter Archived
* Matter Reopened
